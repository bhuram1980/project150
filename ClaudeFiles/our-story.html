May 2026. I sat across from my parents and opened their blood reports — the kind of moment that looks ordinary until it isn't.

My father is 74. My mother is 66. Between them, they've lived through decades of Telugu home cooking, late nights, irregular sleep, and the quiet accumulation of small habits that nobody questions until a doctor finally does.

The reports were not catastrophic. But they were clear. The numbers didn't leave much room for "we'll see."

What the reports showed · May 2026
High blood sugar — pre-diabetic range
Low iron — fatigue, low stamina
Low Vitamin D — bone health risk
Heart risk indicators — needed monitoring

Most families would nod, fill the prescriptions, and move on. We almost did. But something about seeing both their reports side by side — one page, two lives — made it impossible to look away.

I'm not a doctor. I'm not a nutritionist. I'm not a fitness trainer. I'm a son who knows how to read a spreadsheet, and has spent enough time around health data to know that these numbers are not fixed.

They can be moved. With consistency. With discipline. With time.

The Compound

"We didn't join a gym. We built one."

Steel frame. Corrugated roof. Red and black machines. An LED sign at the entrance that says PROJECT 150. Built the same week we read the blood reports.

No coaching No products No memberships Just the truth

That's when Project 150 was born. The idea is simple: my parents' ages today add up to 140. We want to add 10 more — ten more years of healthy, independent life — to bring the combined total to 150. Not just years. Quality years. Years where they walk without pain, sleep through the night, and don't depend on others for the basics.

We are not doing anything dramatic. No extreme diets. No supplements stacked up on the counter. Just traditional Telugu food made more carefully, walks that happen every single day, medicines taken on time, and honest tracking of what changes.

I'm documenting it here because I suspect we are not the only family looking at those numbers and wondering what to do. If this helps even one other family begin — every entry on this site is worth it.
