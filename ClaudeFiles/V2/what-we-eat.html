No special diets. Traditional Telugu home cooking — more carefully prepared, honestly portioned, strictly timed.

Daily Structure

What a Day
Looks Like

Morning
06:30 AM
Light Start · Walk First

Warm water with raw ginger. Walk first — no food until done. After: one fruit, soaked almonds, black tea without sugar.

Lunch
12:30 PM
The Main Meal

Two small rotis or small bowl of brown rice. Dal or sambar — lentils non-negotiable. One vegetable curry, preferably leafy greens. Small bowl of curd. No second helpings.

Evening
05:00 PM
Small · No Packets

Light rasam or vegetable soup. Roasted peanuts or chana. No biscuits, no packaged snacks, no fried items. This rule has no exceptions — not for guests, not for festivals.

Dinner
07:30 PM
Light · Before 8 PM Always

One roti or small khichdi. One steamed vegetable dish. No rice at dinner. Eating finishes strictly by 8 PM. Short 10-minute walk after if possible.

Non-Negotiables

House
Rules

  • I
    No sugary drinks, everNo juice, no sweetened chai, no soft drinks. Water or plain tea only.
  • II
    No packaged snacks in the houseIf it comes in a packet with a shelf life, it doesn't come home.
  • III
    Fixed meal timings — alwaysSame time every day. Consistency is the discipline, not restriction.
  • IV
    Protein and fiber at every mealDal, curd, lentils, or eggs. Greens or sabzi. Every plate, every day.
  • V
    8 glasses of water dailyTracked. One glass before each meal makes it easier to hit the target.
