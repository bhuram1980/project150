Not expert opinions. Lived experience — shared plainly.

What Works
◈ I
Consistency beats intensity

A 30-minute walk every day is worth more than a 2-hour walk twice a week. The habit is the medicine. When my father missed a day due to rain, the next morning he was up earlier than usual. That is the habit working.

◈ II
Family accountability works

When both parents are doing it together, skipping feels harder. The social contract of the household is the strongest discipline tool we have. Neither wants to be the one who broke the rule today.

◈ III
Simple food is sustainable

We haven't introduced a single new ingredient. The same Telugu kitchen — just prepared more carefully, portioned more honestly. Sustainability comes from familiarity, not novelty.

◈ IV
Walking is medicine

After four weeks, both parents report sleeping better and feeling less stiff in the mornings. No supplement produced this. No prescription either. Walking has.

Still Improving
◌ I
Sleep timing is inconsistent

Both parents still go to sleep at different times on different nights. The hardest habit to fix because it doesn't feel urgent until it accumulates. No system for this yet.

◌ II
Social events break discipline

Weddings, gatherings, guests — the food rules collapse immediately. We haven't found a clean answer. The honest position is: we haven't solved it, so we don't pretend we have.

◌ III
Report anxiety is real

Both parents get anxious before blood tests. This anxiety is itself a health variable. Naming it, talking about it openly, helps more than pretending it doesn't exist.

◌ IV
Tracking drops in busy weeks

When life gets loud — family visits, health scares, seasonal stress — the daily tracking is the first thing to go. The habit survives; the documentation doesn't. Working on making it smaller and more automatic.
