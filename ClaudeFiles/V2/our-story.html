May 2026. I sat across from my parents and opened their blood reports — the kind of moment that looks ordinary until it isn't.

My father is 74. My mother is 66. Between them, they've lived through decades of Telugu home cooking, late nights, irregular sleep, and the quiet accumulation of small habits that nobody questions until a doctor finally does.

The reports were not catastrophic. But they were clear. The numbers didn't leave much room for "we'll see."

High blood sugar — pre-diabetic range
Low iron — fatigue, low stamina
Low Vitamin D — bone health risk
Heart risk indicators — needed monitoring

Most families would nod, fill the prescriptions, and move on. We almost did. But something about seeing both reports side by side — one page, two lives — made it impossible to look away.

I'm not a doctor. I'm not a nutritionist. I'm not a fitness trainer. I'm a son who knows how to read a spreadsheet, and has spent enough time around health data to know that these numbers are not fixed. They can be moved. With consistency. With discipline. With time.

That's when Project 150 was born. My parents' ages add up to 140. We want to add ten more — ten more years of healthy, independent life — to bring the combined total to 150.

I'm documenting it here because I suspect we are not the only family looking at those numbers and wondering what to do. If this helps even one other family begin — every entry is worth it.
