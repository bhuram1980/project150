No special diets. Traditional Telugu home cooking — made more carefully, portioned honestly, timed strictly.

Daily Structure

What a
Day Looks Like

Morning
6:30 AM
Light Start · Walk First

Warm water with raw ginger before anything. Walk first — no food until done. After the walk: one fruit (guava, papaya, or banana), a handful of soaked almonds, black tea without sugar.

Lunch
12:30 PM
The Main Meal

Two small rotis or a small bowl of brown rice. One portion of dal or sambar — lentils non-negotiable at every meal. One vegetable curry, preferably leafy greens or gourd family. A small bowl of curd. No second helpings.

Evening
5:00 PM
Small · No Packets

Light rasam or vegetable soup. A small handful of roasted peanuts or chana. No biscuits, no packaged snacks, no fried items. This rule has no exceptions — not for guests, not for festivals.

Dinner
7:30 PM
Light · Always Before 8 PM

One roti or small portion of khichdi. One vegetable dish — steamed or lightly sautéed. No rice at dinner. Eating finishes strictly by 8 PM. A short 10-minute walk after dinner if possible.

Non-Negotiables

House
Rules

  • I
    No sugary drinks, ever No juice, no sweetened chai, no soft drinks. Water or plain tea only. No exceptions.
  • II
    No packaged snacks in the house If it comes in a packet with a shelf life, it doesn't come home. The rule is the boundary.
  • III
    Fixed meal timings — always Same time every day. Consistency is the discipline, not restriction.
  • IV
    Protein and fiber at every meal Dal, curd, lentils, or eggs. Greens or sabzi. Every single plate, every single day.
  • V
    8 glasses of water daily Tracked. One glass before each meal makes it easier to hit the target.
