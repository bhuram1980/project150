06

Usage Rules

✓ Always Do
→Keep "150" in red (#E30613) everywhere it appears in the logo
→Use Playfair Display italic for pull quotes and emotional headings
→Keep backgrounds warm cream — never cold grey or pure white
→Use real numbers, dates, and specifics in all content
→Report setbacks as honestly as wins
✕ Never Do
—Add any selling, coaching, or membership language
—Use red for anything other than the logo "150" mark
—Use stock images — only real family photos or no photos
—Make transformation claims or medical promises
—Stretch, recolour, or rearrange the logo elements
07

Visual Style

Theme

Warm editorial magazine. Light cream background. Not cold, not clinical. Feels like a premium print journal.

Cards

Mix of white, cream, ink-black, and red cards. Variety creates visual contrast without needing photos. Subtle hover lift on each card.

Photography

Real family photos only — parents walking, home meals, blood reports, cooking. Warm natural light. Never posed, never stock.

Inspired By
Kinfolk Magazine — editorial warmth, family focus
The Athletic — sports journalism typography
Linear.app — bento grid structure, precision
Apple — one message per section, no waste
Key Differentiator

Most health and wellness sites use dark themes, aggressive colors, or stock imagery. Project 150 uses warm cream, editorial serif type, and real family content — making it immediately distinct and trustworthy.
